â€¢ Random Sampling: From each stratum, participants are randomly selected to participate in the study. This ensures that each stratum is represented proportionally or equally, depending on the study design.

â€¢ Control Over Confounding: By stratifying the population by known confounders, the impact of these confounders is controlled across the treatment groups, reducing their effect on the outcome variable.
